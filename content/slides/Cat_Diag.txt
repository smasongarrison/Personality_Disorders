\begin{frame}[fragile,allowframebreaks]{General Personality Disorder Criteria}
\begin{itemize} \item An enduring pattern of inner experience and behavior that deviates markedly from the expectations of the individualâ€™s culture. This pattern is manifested in two (or more) of the following areas:\begin{enumerate}
\item Cognition (i.e., ways of perceiving and interpreting self, other people, and events).
\item Affectivity (i.e.,  the range,  intensity,  lability, and appropriateness of emotional  response).
\item Interpersonal functioning.
\item Impulse control.\end{enumerate}\\~\\
\item The enduring pattern is inflexible and pervasive across a broad range of personal and social situations.
\item The enduring pattern leads to clinically significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning.\\~\\
\item The pattern is stable and of long duration, and its onset can be traced back at least to  adolescence or early adulthood.\\~\\
\item The enduring pattern is not better explained as a manifestation or consequence of another mental disorder.\\~\\
\item The enduring pattern is not attributable to the physiological effects of a substance (e.g., a drug of abuse, a medication) or another medical condition (e.g., head trauma).
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
